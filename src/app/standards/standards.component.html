Standards

Each grade level has standards that are organized around topics, with each addressing one topic. Topics are interconnected and a deeper understanding at higher grades requires foundational understanding of many of these topics.

There are indicators that reflect the three dimensions of science learning, they each include a disciplinary core idea, a crosscutting concept, and a science and engineering practice.

Disciplinary core ideas are focused and limited set of ideas that have been identified as necessary for all students for their education. Crosscutting concepts are used to organize and understand the disciplinary core ideas. They help bridge disciplinary boundaries and help deepen the understanding of science content. Science and Engineering practices are used by students to demonstrate their understanding of disciplinary core ideas and crosscutting concepts.
